The Red Fort (Lal Qila) in Delhi is a magnificent symbol of India's rich history and architectural brilliance. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648, this imposing fort is constructed from striking red sandstone, giving it its iconic name. Serving as the main residence of Mughal rulers for nearly 200 years, the fort showcases a perfect blend of Persian, Timurid, and Indian architectural styles.

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a breathtaking symbol of royal elegance and architectural splendor in Karnataka, India.
